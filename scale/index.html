UNIVERSE

Let us begin our journey of scale with the biggest entity known to mankind

"The Universe".

Scientists measure the size of the universe in a myriad of different ways. The size of the Universe is somewhat difficult to define. According to the general theory of relativity, some regions of space may never interact with ours even in the lifetime of the Universe due to the finite speed of light and the ongoing expansion of space. Because we cannot observe space beyond the edge of the observable universe. Estimates for the total size of the universe, if finite, reach as high as 101010122 megaparsecs ...

SOLAR SYSTEM

We now reduce our scale to another big entity

"Our Solar System"

Defining the size of the Solar System is a hard thing to do because it does not have a clear boundary, but considering all the comets visible.. We can estimate the size of our solar system. We know 1 light year is 63,270 AU, so The Solar System has a diameter of about 100,000 AU. A big size indeed!

ECOSYSTEM

Our journey has now gotten us to

"Ecosystem".

The various ecosystems around us, each comprising of many different living and non-living entity are extremely diverse in nature. These entities range from a few millimeters to a few kilometers

CELLS

We now come towards the minute range of sizes.

"Cells"

the basic structural, functional, and biological unit of all known living organisms. A cell is the smallest unit of life. Cells are often called the "building blocks of life". These vital entities are so small yet so important. They range from dimensions between 1 and 100 micrometres.

ATOM

We now come to the final building block of everything. The smallest enitity

"Atom".

An atom is the smallest constituent unit of ordinary matter that has the properties of a chemical element. Every solid, liquid, gas, and plasma is composed of neutral or ionized atoms. Atoms are extremely small; typical sizes are around 100 picometers.
